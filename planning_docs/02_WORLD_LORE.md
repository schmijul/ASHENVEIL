# ASHENVEIL - World & Lore

## The Aether

The Aether was a visible energy network flowing through the sky like luminous rivers. It connected three realms and powered everything: magic, communication, seasons, agriculture. Aethersmiths could shape it into tools, weapons, and infrastructure.

**200 years ago: The Extinction Event.** The Aether shattered without warning. Nobody knows why. Since then:

- The Aether crystallizes on contact with surfaces, creating toxic crystal formations across the landscape
- Prolonged Aether exposure mutates animals into aggressive, twisted creatures
- Humans exposed too long develop "Aether Madness" — paranoia, aggression, eventual death
- The three realms lost all contact with each other

**Aether Crystals** are the solidified remains. They still hold immense power but are dangerous to handle. Aethersmiths — the rare few who can work crystals without going mad — are the most valuable people alive.

## The Three Realms

### Kernwall (Military Fortress State)

- **Geography:** Mountain plateau, surrounded by walls made of fused Aether crystal
- **Culture:** Militaristic, hierarchical, paranoid. Strength is respect.
- **Government:** General-Commander, elected by combat officers
- **Attitude toward Aether:** Weaponize it. Control it. Wall it off.
- **Attitude toward player:** Suspicious. Prove your worth through combat and loyalty.
- **Key resources:** Aether-forged weapons, disciplined soldiers, fortified positions
- **Weakness:** Isolationism is starving them. They need trade but won't admit it.

### Flimmermoor (Swamp Trader Network)

- **Geography:** Vast marshlands with hidden trade routes, stilt-villages, river networks
- **Culture:** Pragmatic, mercantile, morally flexible. Coin talks.
- **Government:** Loose council of merchant families. Power = wealth.
- **Attitude toward Aether:** Sell it. Smuggle it. Profit from it.
- **Attitude toward player:** Useful tool. What can you do for us?
- **Key resources:** Trade routes, information networks, smuggled goods
- **Weakness:** No military. If Kernwall invades, they fold.

### Hohensang (Ruined Scholar City)

- **Geography:** Crumbling university city on a cliff, overgrown with Aether crystal growths
- **Culture:** Was the intellectual center of the world. Now occupied by cults and scavengers.
- **Government:** Competing cult leaders, each claiming to understand the Extinction
- **Government:** Cult of the Rekindling (wants to restart Aether), Hollow Circle (wants to destroy all remaining Aether), various splinter groups
- **Attitude toward Aether:** Worship it. Study it. Become one with it.
- **Attitude toward player:** You can touch Aether? You're either a messiah or a threat.
- **Key resources:** Ancient knowledge, Aether research, powerful but unstable magic
- **Weakness:** Internal conflict. They'll destroy themselves before any enemy needs to.

## The Player Character

- Wakes up unconscious at the edge of the village Grauweiler after an Aether storm
- No memory of identity or past
- Hands glow faintly — can touch Aether without corruption (unique in 200 years)
- Adopted by the village as a hunter to earn keep
- True nature is revealed gradually through the story

## Why This Works For The Native Build

- The lore is built around a grounded third-person prolog, so the new Godot camera and movement model can stay close to the Gothic reference.
- Grauweiler functions as the first playable social hub, which keeps dialogue, trading, and quest setup tightly focused.
- The three realms provide long-term scale, but the first native slice only needs enough world context to make the prolog and Aether escalation believable.

## Central Conflict

Someone — or something — is deliberately trying to restart the Aether Flow. This could:
- **Heal the world:** Reconnect the realms, end the mutations, restore civilization
- **Destroy it:** An uncontrolled restart could cause a second Extinction, worse than the first

Each realm has a different plan for the restart. The player decides whose plan (if any) succeeds.

## Key Themes

- Power and its cost (Aether corruption mechanic mirrors this)
- Isolation vs. connection (three realms that need each other but won't cooperate)
- Identity (amnesiac protagonist, "who am I" through player choice)
- No clear good/evil — every faction has valid reasons and ugly methods
